Digital Cities

Digitization of health and welfare services

The digitization of health- and welfare services can be a means of spending scarce resources more efficiently. Digital services can also provide flexibility so that clients and patients will be better able to integrate treatment and welfare programs in their daily life. At the same time, there are challenges connected to the digitization in the health- and welfare sector.In the first video, Anders Underthun, senior researcher at the Work Research Institute at OsloMet, will present a case of how home care services have been digitized in a Norwegian municipality. He will show how the introduction of digital work-lists makes it easier for the nurses to meet the varying needs of their patients, and also point out some challenges that arise when face-to-face communication with colleagues is replaced with digital communication. Digitization does not necessarily imply turning an existing paper-based system into a digital replica. The following video will present how NAV, the Norwegian Labour and Welfare Administration, approaches digitization as an opportunity for transformation.

Innovation in the digital city

Cities are arenas of formal and casual interaction between different professions, different companies and organizations, and different groups of people. This can provide a fertile ground for innovation. What is innovation? Tor Paaske Utheim at Department of Research and Development, OsloMet, will give an answer to this in the first video. Tor Paaske Utheim defines innovation as something that is new, useful and implemented. Think about the rapid digitalization in response to the corona crisis. Do you have an example of something that can be categorized as an innovation in this context? Why can this be labeled an innovation?
Good ideas, access to collaboration partners, and access to financing are important elements in an innovation process. Another crucial element is insight about the potential users and the use context. In the following video, Max Tangen, CEO in 7Sense Products, will explain how his company has used the method 'design driven innovation' to develop relevant and attractive products for their customers.Think about your field of study, future profession, or another field that you are interested in. How could design driven innovation be used to generate better products or services in this context?
Every now and then a new technology brings about profound societal changes. Is the electric scooter a radical innovation with the capacity to change urban living significantly? Hanne Cecilie Geirbo, Associate Professor at Department of Computer Science, OsloMet, will speculate on this by looking at the electric scooters through the lenses of transport, data and machine learning, universal design, and sustainability.

Top 5 smartest cities in the world

Singapore

Singapore is a global economic hub celebrated for its cleanliness, cultural diversity, and culinary delights. Its iconic skyline, technological innovation, and vibrant shopping scene make it a dynamic and enticing city-state.

Zürich

Zurich is renowned for its status as Switzerland's financial hub, nestled by Lake Zurich and surrounded by the Alps. It captivates with its picturesque old town, cultural richness, and as a global center for banking and finance.

Oslo

Oslo, the capital of Norway, is celebrated for its stunning waterfront, surrounded by hills and forests. It's known for its modern architecture, vibrant cultural scene, and as a gateway to outdoor adventures in the fjords and beyond.

Taipei City

Taipei City, the vibrant capital of Taiwan, is renowned for its towering skyscrapers, including the iconic Taipei 101. It's a blend of modern and traditional, boasting bustling night markets, cultural temples, and a rich culinary scene.

Lausanne

Lausanne is renowned for its picturesque setting and as the headquarters of the International Olympic Committee. This Swiss city combines historic charm with modern elegance, offering stunning views of the lake and the Alps.
